# Portfolio Masterplan
### AI Engineer & Founder building automation that actually ships

---

## Overview & Objective

This portfolio is designed as a **sales funnel** — every section earns the next scroll. The visitor arrives curious, builds trust through your story, sees undeniable proof through your work, and leaves knowing exactly how to reach you.

**Primary audience:** Recruiters and hiring managers
**Secondary audience:** Potential clients looking for AI automation expertise
**Future audience:** Graduate admissions committees

**Core identity:** A 19-year-old AI engineer and founder who doesn't just learn about AI — he builds real systems that ship, scale, and solve problems.

---

## Audience Snapshot

| Audience | What They Care About | What Convinces Them |
|----------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Recruiters | Skills, problem-solving ability, impact, initiative | Projects that show depth + the fact you've been building since 15 |
| Clients | Can this person solve MY problem? | Real automated systems, clear results, professional maturity |
| Grad Admissions | Academic depth, research potential, growth trajectory | Technical projects, journey of learning, ambition |

---

## Site Structure — The Funnel

The portfolio follows a deliberate **Hook → Story → Proof → Action** flow.

---

### Section 1: Hero — The Hook

**Goal:** Stop the scroll. Make them want to know more.

**Content:**
- Headline: *"AI Engineer & Founder building automation that actually ships"*
- A 1-2 line subheadline that adds texture — something like: *"I've been building since I was 15. From e-commerce to AI automation — I turn ideas into systems that work."*
- Two clear call-to-action buttons:
  - "See My Work" → scrolls to Projects
  - "Get In Touch" → scrolls to Contact
- A subtle visual element or animation that reinforces the "cutting-edge" energy — think clean, bold, and modern

**Vibe:** Confident, not arrogant. Bold, not loud. The visitor should immediately think: *"This person is serious."*

---

### Section 2: About / Journey — Build Connection

**Goal:** Turn curiosity into trust. Show the human behind the work.

**Content approach:** Start with who you are NOW, then weave in the journey.

**Structure:**
- **Open with the present:** Lead with your identity as an AI Engineer and Founder. You build automated systems that solve real problems. You lead a 5-person agency. You're relentlessly curious and always shipping.
- **Then the journey:** Started building at 15 with e-commerce. Learned marketing the hard way when international dropshipping failed. That failure pushed you deeper into tech. Built a food delivery service that hit 500+ users. Discovered AI automation and never looked back. Now you're engineering systems that generate content, personalize outreach, and even run LLMs in impossibly tight constraints.
- **The thread that ties it all together:** You've always been someone who sees a problem and builds the solution — whether that's a campus without food delivery or an education system without localized AI.

**Tone:** Honest, forward-moving, shows growth. Not a resume — a story.

**Do NOT mention:** Specific university name, countries lived in, or GPA.

---

### Section 3: What I Do — Skills at a Glance

**Goal:** Quick-scan section for recruiters who want to know your capabilities fast.

**Content — organized by category:**

- **AI & Machine Learning:** Agentic RAG, LLM integration, neural networks, K-means clustering, RNNs, sentiment analysis, computer vision
- **Automation & Systems:** n8n workflow automation, API integrations, end-to-end pipeline design, email automation
- **Web Development:** Flask, FastAPI, Django, Python, HTML/CSS
- **AI Tools & Platforms:** HeyGen, Submagic, FFmpeg, Google APIs
- **Soft Skills:** Client communication, bridging technical and business, team leadership, sales

**Format:** Visual and scannable — think skill cards or grouped icons, not a wall of text. Keep it tight and let the Projects section do the heavy lifting.

---

### Section 4: Projects — The Proof

**Goal:** This is where the visitor becomes a believer. Each project should tell a mini-story: *What's the problem? What did you build? Why does it matter?*

**Project framework for each card:**
- Project name
- One-line description (the hook)
- The problem it solved
- What you built (your approach)
- Why it matters (the "so what?")
- Tech used (small, secondary — tags or badges)

---

#### Project 1: FBISE RAG Chatbot
*The first AI education chatbot built for Pakistan's federal board*

- **Problem:** LLMs are trained on generic British/American syllabi — Pakistani students using AI for studying get wrong or irrelevant answers
- **What you built:** An agentic RAG chatbot with safety guardrails, trained on FBISE-specific curriculum content
- **Why it matters:** 120+ real users. You identified a gap no one was filling and built a product people actually used
- **Tech:** Agentic RAG, LLMs, safety guardrails

---

#### Project 2: LLM Inference on xv6
*Running an LLM inside a 128MB RAM operating system*

- **Problem:** Can you make LLM inference work under extreme hardware constraints?
- **What you built:** Integrated LLM inference into the xv6 teaching OS, working within just 128MB of RAM
- **Why it matters:** This is deep systems-level engineering. It shows you understand what's happening under the hood, not just at the API layer
- **Tech:** xv6, C, LLM inference, memory optimization

---

#### Project 3: AI Content Pipeline
*Fully automated video content creation — from competitor research to final edit*

This is an umbrella project with three interconnected systems:

**Phase 1 — Smart Scripting Engine**
- Analyzed competitors' recent reels, identified the most viral content, and generated scripts that mirror the personality of a cloned creator
- Automated: competitor extraction → viral pattern detection → personality-matched script generation

**Phase 2 — AI Video Production**
- Cloned the creator's audio and video, then used AI to automatically edit the final output
- Automated: voice cloning → video cloning → AI editing

**Phase 3 — Clip Extraction & Editing**
- Extracted highlight clips from longer videos and auto-edited them with AI
- Automated: clip detection → extraction → AI-powered editing

- **Why it matters:** This isn't "I used an AI tool." This is "I built an entire automated production system." It shows systems thinking, API orchestration, and real-world pipeline architecture
- **Tech:** n8n, HeyGen, Submagic, FFmpeg, Google APIs

---

#### Project 4: Cal AI Clone
*A calorie estimation app built from scratch using ML*

- **Problem:** Can you replicate a commercial AI product using fundamental ML techniques?
- **What you built:** A calorie estimation system using K-means clustering and neural networks — a ground-up clone of Cal AI
- **Why it matters:** Shows strong ML fundamentals and the ability to reverse-engineer and rebuild a product
- **Tech:** K-means, Neural Networks, Python

---

#### Project 5: Stock Prediction with RNN
*Predicting stock prices using recurrent neural networks and live sentiment analysis*

- **Problem:** Stock prediction using only historical price data is limited — market sentiment matters
- **What you built:** An RNN-based prediction model that incorporates live sentiment analysis for more informed predictions
- **Why it matters:** Combines deep learning with real-time data integration — shows you can build systems that respond to the real world
- **Tech:** RNN, Sentiment Analysis, Python

---

#### Project 6: AI Custom Emails
*Personalized outreach at scale — fully automated*

- **Problem:** Generic mass emails get ignored — 0.001% reply rate
- **What you built:** An automated system that researches each recipient individually, generates personalized emails, and handles follow-ups — all without manual intervention
- **Why it matters:** Improved reply rate from 0.001% to 3.5% through smarter targeting. Shows you understand iteration — you built it, measured it, and made it dramatically better
- **Tech:** AI personalization, email automation, automated follow-ups

## Color & Typography Guidelines

**Vibe:** Bold, cutting-edge, innovative — but clean and professional. Not cluttered.

- **Color direction:** Dark theme as the base (deep navy or near-black) with a bold accent color (electric blue, neon green, or a vibrant gradient). This reinforces the "ahead of the curve" energy.
- **Typography direction:** A modern sans-serif for headings (bold, high impact) paired with a clean readable font for body text. Big headlines, generous spacing.
- **Imagery:** Minimal but intentional. Abstract tech visuals, subtle animations, or geometric patterns. No stock photos. Let the content breathe.
